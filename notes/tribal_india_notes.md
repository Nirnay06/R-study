## 1. Concept of Tribe

- European anthropologists originally used "tribe" to describe "primitive" or "barbaric" people lacking writing.
- **Adivasi** = generic Indian term.
- Tribal society is also called: *primitive society, folk society, simple society, preliterate society.*
- Anthropologically: a tribe is a **social group** sharing:
  - Common territory, dialect, ancestor, uniform social organization, simple technology, cultural homogeneity.
  - Eg: **Onge** of Andaman Islands.

### Key Definitions
| Scholar | Definition |
|---|---|
| **D.N. Majumdar** | Social group with territorial affiliation, endogamous, no functional specialization, ruled by tribal officers (hereditary/otherwise), united by language/dialect, recognizes social distance with other tribes/castes |
| **Imperial Gazetteer of India** | Group of families with common name, common dialect, reside in common area, observe marriage taboos |

## 2. Scheduled Tribes (Constitutional Provisions)

- **Article 366(25):** Defines Scheduled Tribes as specified under Article 342.
- **Article 342:** The President (through public notification, after consultation with Governor) specifies which tribes are STs for each state.
- ST status grants constitutional benefits and protections.

### Lokur Committee Criteria for ST Status
1. Primitive traits
2. Distinctive culture
3. Geographical isolation
4. Shyness of contact with larger community
5. Backwardness

> **Case Study — Narikuravar:** Nomadic community denied ST status for long because their active interaction with wider society didn't fit "shyness of contact" criteria. Finally granted ST status in **2023** after long struggle.

## 3. Features of Tribal Life (10 Key Features)

| # | Feature | Key Point | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Geographical Isolation** | Remote/inaccessible areas; depend on forest resources | Jarawa (Andaman Islands) |
| 2 | **Homogeneous Ethnic Group** | Distinct cultural identity; strong unity | Bhil (Rajasthan) |
| 3 | **Definite Territory** | Specific geographic area | Toda (Nilgiri Hills, Tamil Nadu) |
| 4 | **Common Language & Culture** | Own language/dialect, folklore | Gond (Central India) |
| 5 | **Low Level of Technology** | Simple traditional tools | Baiga (Madhya Pradesh) — digging sticks, bows |
| 6 | **Low Literacy** | Limited education access; exceptions exist | Mizoram (high literacy); Birhor (Jharkhand) — low |
| 7 | **Endogamous Marriage** | Marriage within tribe to preserve cultural purity | Santhal (West Bengal) |
| 8 | **Direct Personal Relationships** | Close communal bonds | Bonda (Odisha) |
| 9 | **Division of Labor by Age & Sex** | Work split by gender/age | Khasi (Meghalaya): men=agriculture, women=weaving |
| 10 | **Governed by Customary Laws** | Unwritten traditional laws; elder councils | Naga (Nagaland) |

## 4. Classification of Tribes

### A) Territorial Distribution (by B.S. Guha — first 3 zones; 4th added later)

| Zone | States | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **North/North-East Zone** | Assam, Manipur, Tripura, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Nagaland, Sikkim | Nagas, Garo, Khasi, Lepchas (Sikkim), Kuki, Toto (W. Bengal) |
| **Central/Middle Zone** | UP, MP, Maharashtra, Rajasthan, Gujarat, Bihar | Mundas, Santhals, Baiga, Oraons, Ho, Birhor |
| **Southern Zone** | Kerala, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, AP, Telangana | Irulas, Kotas, Todas (Nilgiris), Yerukula, Yanadi, Chenchu, Kurumbas, Koyas |
| **Island Zone** | Andaman & Nicobar, Lakshadweep | Onge, Sentinelese |

### B) Racial Affinity / Bio-genetic Variability

| Race | Physical Traits | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Negrito** | Short/medium stature, dark brown skin, flat-broad nose, thick lips, woolly hair | Onge, Jarawa, Sentinelese, Great Andamanese |
| **Proto-Australoid** | Medium stature, dark skin, curly hair, low forehead, muscular build; also called *pre-Dravidian*; **LARGEST tribal group in India** | Chenchu, Koya, Yerukula, Gond, Santhal |
| **Mongoloid** | Medium stature, yellowish-brown skin, flat face, prominent cheekbones, flat nose, oblique eye-slit with epicanthic fold, straight hair (minimal body/facial hair) | Garo, Kuki, Naga |
| **Other groups** | Mediterranean (Paleo/Mediterranean/Oriental), Western Brachycephals (Alpinoid/Dinaric/Armenoid), Nordic | Diverse tribes not fitting above |

### C) Other Classification Bases

- **Linguistic affiliation**

## 5. Bio-genetic Variability

- Definition: Genetic differences among human populations arising from **gene-environment interaction**.
- Reflected in: blood groups, hemoglobin types, physical features.
- In tribes: reflects long-term evolutionary processes, isolation, migration, adaptation.

### Scholarly Studies
| Scholar | Study | Finding |
|---|---|---|
| **Risley, Guha, Sarkar** | Racial/Ethnic classification | (See Indian Society module) |
| **___ et al. (mtDNA Study)** | 115 individuals across linguistic groups | Austro-Asiatic tribes > greater genetic diversity than Dravidian tribes → **older ancestry** |
| **___ et al. (1985)** | Vasava & Kotwalia tribes, Surat District, Gujarat | No major genetic difference within same ethnic group in same region → **regional homogeneity** due to endogamy |
| **___ et al. (1981)** | 11 major tribal groups of Andhra Pradesh (Chenchus, Pradhans) | Geographically close tribes share genetic similarities → **regional gene flow** |

> Key conclusion: Indian tribes = **living laboratory of human genetic variation**

## EXAM-READY POINTS

### Tribe vs Scheduled Tribe (common question)
- **Tribe** = anthropological/sociological concept based on shared culture, territory, language.
- **Scheduled Tribe** = legal/constitutional concept — listed by President under Article 342.
- Not all tribes are STs; not all STs are "tribes" in strict anthropological sense.
- Lokur Committee gave 5 criteria (not in legislation but "well established").

### Important Scholars to Remember
- **D.N. Majumdar** — definition of tribe
- **B.S. Guha** — territorial classification (3 zones)
- **Lokur Committee** — criteria for ST status
