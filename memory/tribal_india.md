## 1. Concept of Tribe

- European Anthropologists used "tribe" to refer to "primitive" or "barbaric" people lacking art of writing.
- **Adivasi** = generic Indian term.
- Tribal society is also called: *primitive society, folk society, simple society, preliterate society.*
- Anthropologically: a tribe is a **social group** sharing common territory, dialect, ancestor, uniform social organization, simple technology, and cultural homogeneity.
  - Eg: **Onge** of Andaman Islands.

**Definitions:**

| Scholar | Definition |
|---|---|
| **D.N. Majumdar** | Social group with territorial affiliation, endogamous, no specialization of functions, ruled by tribal officers (hereditary or otherwise), united by language/dialect, recognizes social distance with other tribes/castes. |
| **Imperial Gazetteer of India** | Group of families with a common name, members speak a dialect, reside in a common area, observe taboo in marriage (though they may not have observed this taboo originally). |

## 2. Scheduled Tribes — Constitutional Provisions

- **Article 366(25):** Defines Scheduled Tribes as specified under Article 342.
- **Article 342:** The President, through public notification (after consultation with Governor), specifies which tribes are STs for each state.
- ST status grants constitutional benefits and protections.

**Key distinction:**
- **Tribe** = anthropological/sociological concept (shared culture, territory, language).
- **Scheduled Tribe** = legal/constitutional concept — listed by the President under Article 342.
- Not all tribes are STs; not all STs are "tribes" in the strict anthropological sense.

**Lokur Committee Criteria for ST Status** (not in legislation but "well established"):
1. Primitive traits (P)
2. Distinctive culture (D)
3. Geographical isolation (G)
4. Shyness of contact with community at large (S)
5. Backwardness (B)
- Mnemonic: **BDS PG**

**Case Study — Narikuravar** (studied by Majumdar):
- Nomadic community; despite nomadic lifestyle and social marginalization, their *active interaction* with wider society made it hard to meet "geographical isolation" and "shyness of contact" criteria.
- Delayed recognition → poor living conditions, limited government benefits, social exclusion.
- Finally granted ST status in **2023** after a long struggle.
- Highlights the complexities of defining and recognizing tribal identity in India's diverse social landscape.

## 3. Features of Tribal Life (10 Key Features)

| # | Feature | Key Point | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Geographical Isolation** | Remote/inaccessible areas; depend on natural/forest resources | Jarawa (Andaman Islands) |
| 2 | **Homogeneous Ethnic Group** | Distinct cultural identity (food, dress, customs, beliefs); strong unity | Bhil (Rajasthan) |
| 3 | **Definite Territory** | Specific geographical area; sense of territorial belonging | Toda (Nilgiri Hills, Tamil Nadu) |
| 4 | **Common Language & Culture** | Own language/dialect; strengthens identity | Gond (Central India) |
| 5 | **Low Level of Technology** | Simple traditional tools; tied to local environment | Baiga (MP) — digging sticks, bows |
| 6 | **Low Literacy** | Limited education access; exceptions exist | Mizoram (high); Birhor (Jharkhand) — low |
| 7 | **Endogamous Marriage** | Marriage within tribe to preserve cultural purity | Santhal (West Bengal) |
| 8 | **Direct Personal Relationships** | Close communal bonds; collective participation | Bonda (Odisha) |
| 9 | **Division of Labor by Age & Sex** | Work split by gender/age | Khasi (Meghalaya): men=agriculture, women=weaving |
| 10 | **Governed by Customary Laws** | Unwritten traditional laws; elder councils | Naga (Nagaland) |

## 4. Classification of Tribes

**Bases:** Territorial distribution | Racial affinity | Occupation specialization | Linguistic affiliation

### A) Territorial Distribution (B.S. Guha — first 3 zones; 4th added later)

| Zone | States | Tribe Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **North/North-East Zone** | Assam, Manipur, Tripura, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Nagaland, Sikkim | Nagas, Garo, Khasi, Lepchas (Sikkim), Kuki, Toto (W. Bengal) |
| **Central/Middle Zone** | UP, MP, Maharashtra, Rajasthan, Gujarat, Bihar | Mundas, Santhals, Baiga, Oraons, Ho, Birhor |
| **Southern Zone** | Kerala, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, AP, Telangana | Irulas, Kotas (Nilgiris), Todas, Yerukula, Yanadi, Chenchu, Kurumbas, Koyas |
| **Island Zone** *(later addition)* | Andaman & Nicobar, Lakshadweep | Onge, Sentinelese |

### B) Racial Affinity / Bio-genetic Variability (4 Racial Groups)

| Race | Physical Traits | Tribe Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Negrito** | Short/medium stature, dark brown skin, flat-broad nose, thick lips, woolly hair | Onge, Jarawa, Sentinelese, Great Andamanese |
| **Proto-Australoid** | Medium stature, dark skin, curly hair, low forehead, muscular; also called *pre-Dravidian*; **LARGEST tribal group in India** | Chenchu, Koya, Yerukula, Gond, Santhal |
| **Mongoloid** | Medium stature, yellowish-brown skin, flat face, prominent cheekbones, flat nose, oblique eye-slit with epicanthic fold, straight hair (minimal body/facial hair) | Garo, Kuki, Naga |
| **Other groups** | Mediterranean (Paleo/Mediterranean/Oriental), Western Brachycephals (Alpinoid/Dinaric/Armenoid), Nordic | Tribes not fitting above 3 groups |

## 5. Bio-genetic Variability

**Definition:** Genetic differences among human populations arising from **gene-environment interaction**. Produces variability in blood groups, hemoglobin types, and physical features.

In tribal populations: reflects long-term evolutionary processes, isolation, migration, and adaptation to diverse ecological zones.

**Scholarly Studies:**

| Scholar | Study | Finding |
|---|---|---|
| Risley, Guha, Sarkar | Racial/Ethnic Classification | (Refer Indian Society module) |
| **Majumdar et al.** (mtDNA Study) | mtDNA across 115 individuals from different linguistic groups | Austro-Asiatic tribes → **greater genetic diversity** than Dravidian tribes → **older ancestry** |
| **Bhasin et al. (1985)** | Vasava & Kotwalia tribes, Surat District, Gujarat | No major genetic difference within same ethnic group in same region → **regional homogeneity** due to endogamy |
| **Blake et al. (1981)** | 11 major tribal groups of AP (Chenchus, Pradhans) | Geographically close tribes share genetic similarities → **regional gene flow** |

> Key conclusion: Indian tribes = **"living laboratory of human genetic variation"**
